Hospital Patient Management System Using Facial Recognition in Cambodia

Abstract:

In Cambodia, healthcare facilities are increasingly adopting innovative technologies to improve patient management and streamline registration processes. This project introduces a hospital patient management system that leverages facial recognition technology to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of patient information access. The system employs Haar Cascade classifiers for face detection and Local Binary Patterns Histograms (LBPH) for face recognition, providing a robust and reliable solution tailored to the local context.

The proposed system replaces traditional identification methods with a facial recognition approach, significantly reducing registration time and minimizing errors associated with manual data entry. Upon arrival, patients simply stand before a kiosk equipped with a camera that captures their facial features. The Haar Cascade algorithm swiftly detects the face, while the LBPH algorithm performs recognition by comparing the captured image with the stored patient database.

The integration of this technology not only accelerates the registration process but also enhances security by ensuring that only authorized personnel can access sensitive patient information. The system is designed to be user-friendly, with an intuitive interface that guides patients through the recognition process seamlessly.

In conclusion, the implementation of a facial recognition-based patient management system in Cambodian hospitals represents a significant step towards modernizing healthcare services. By utilizing Haar Cascade for face detection and LBPH for face recognition, this project demonstrates the potential for technology to transform patient experiences, improve operational efficiency, and ensure high standards of data security in healthcare settings.
